Coverage Made Understandable

A policy should match your vehicle, driving habits, and budget.

The old page focused on customized protection. This version keeps that idea, but makes the coverage choices easier to scan.

Liability

Helps pay for injury or property damage to others when an accident is deemed your fault.

Collision

Helps cover damage to your own vehicle after a covered wreck.

Comprehensive

Helps with theft, fire, vandalism, weather damage, and other non-collision losses.

Medical Payments / PIP

May help pay medical bills and sometimes related losses after an accident.

Uninsured Motorist

Helps protect you when another driver does not carry enough liability coverage.

Policy Add-ons

Roadside assistance, rental reimbursement, custom parts, replacement cost, vanishing deductibles, and gap coverage.

Price Factors

What can affect your auto insurance cost?

  • Your selected coverage limits and deductibles
  • The value of your vehicle and whether it is financed
  • Your accident, claim, and driving history
  • Where you live and how far you drive each year
